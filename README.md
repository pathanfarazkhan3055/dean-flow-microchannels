# Numerical Study of Dean Flow Formation in Curved Microchannels

## Project Overview

This project presents a computational investigation of Dean flow development in curved microchannels using ANSYS-based CFD simulations.

Dean flow is a secondary flow phenomenon that arises when fluid travels through curved passages, generating counter-rotating vortices due to the combined effects of centrifugal forces and pressure gradients. The study focuses on analyzing and comparing Dean vortex formation in different curved channel geometries.

The work was carried out as part of a fluid mechanics and computational modeling study at the **Indian Institute of Technology Indore**.

---

## Problem Statement

Fluid flow through curved microchannels experiences complex secondary flow structures that significantly influence mixing and transport characteristics.

The challenges addressed in this study include:

* Understanding the formation of Dean vortices in curved channels
* Investigating the influence of Reynolds Number on flow behavior
* Evaluating the effect of Dean Number on vortex strength
* Comparing flow characteristics in different channel geometries
* Identifying the relationship between curvature and secondary flow development

The objective is to numerically analyze Dean flow formation and evaluate the performance of different curved microchannel configurations.

---

## System Model

### U-Bent Microchannel

Three-dimensional CFD model of a U-shaped curved microchannel designed to investigate secondary flow development at the bend section.

### Spiral Microchannel

Three-dimensional CFD model of a spiral-shaped microchannel used to analyze continuous curvature effects on Dean vortex formation.

### Governing Parameters

* Reynolds Number (Re)
* Dean Number (De)
* Velocity Distribution
* Pressure Gradient
* Secondary Flow Intensity

## Results

| Observation              | Outcome                                                      |
| ------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Dean Number Effect       | Vortex strength increased with Dean Number                   |
| Velocity Distribution    | High-velocity regions shifted toward the outer wall          |
| Secondary Flow Formation | Clear Dean vortices observed in curved sections              |
| Geometry Comparison      | U-bent and spiral channels exhibited different flow patterns |
| Mixing Characteristics   | Enhanced secondary circulation improved fluid mixing         |

The numerical simulations successfully captured Dean vortex formation and demonstrated the significant influence of channel geometry and Dean Number on secondary flow behavior.
